I would like to place my name into consideration for the role of
System Administrator for Research Cyberinfrastructure in RCI, 
position number 4M0693.  My career began in software development with an
emphasis on scientific software, then moving into operations
management and systems administration, and when that is combined
with my  working relationships with other system admistrators in
the MSU community, I feel that I have a lot to offer towards that
position.

Upon arriving at the MSU library, I took over administration of six
Rocky Linux 8 servers.  My projects have included migrating 
iptables firewall configurations to firewalld, putting
into place a reliable tape backup system using Amanda (it may be
old software, but it does the job with resources that we have at hand), updating modsecurity rules, 
managing updates,
and currently shepherding a migration of our samba system from local
accounts
to using MSU Active Directory.  On the applications
side, I manage Scholarworks, which is the open access repository
for the intellectual works at MSU.  I've since guided Scholarworks through
5 updates, and ss you are aware, migrating
it from one of the library's rocky 8 servers to new RHEL 9 VMs in
Enterprise Computing.

While at the National Weather Service, I was 24-7 operational support for two web GIS applications, NowCoast and IDP-GIS.  This required writing a lot of python
and bash scripts to plug into the BigBrother monitoring system and constant engagement at the NCEP "OPS" meetings.  I also have
a long history with MATLAB, joining the the Image and
Scientific Data Formats team at the MathWorks in 2007, becoming  team lead
after one year.  This give me a broad experience with a variety of
open source image and scientific data formats and APIs, ranging
from older “classic” formats such as JPEG, FITS, TIFF, and HDF4,
to newer and more complex formats such as JPEG2000 and HDF5/netCDF.
In fact, my first assignment at MathWorks was to implement official
netCDF support in MATLAB, and this was largely based upon the earlier
netCDF community project on SourceForge, MEXCDF, to which I was the
prime contributor.   I also authored the R2011a “high-level” HDF5
interface for MATLAB while continuing to refactor and performance-tune
the existing “low-level” HDF5 interface.

Thank you for your consideration.  Please do not hesitate to contact
me if you require any further information.
